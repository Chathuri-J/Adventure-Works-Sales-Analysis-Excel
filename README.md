Adventure Works Sales Analysis – Excel Dashboard Project

Project Overview
This project presents an **end-to-end sales analysis of the Adventure Works dataset** using **Microsoft Excel**.  
It includes **interactive dashboards** designed to analyze sales performance across **time, products, customers, geography, and profitability**.

The project demonstrates practical skills in:
- Data modeling (fact & dimension tables)
- Business intelligence concepts
- Advanced Excel dashboard design
- Insight-driven storytelling

---

Dataset & Data Model
The Excel workbook is structured using a **star schema**, commonly used in BI systems.

Fact Table
- **factInternetSales**
  - Sales amount, order quantity, profit, COGS
  - Linked to product, customer, date, and territory

Dimension Tables
- **dimProduct** – Product details, categories, colors, pricing
- **dimSalesTerritory** – Sales regions and territories
- **dimDate** – Year, quarter, month, weekday analysis
- **dimCustomer** – Customer demographics
- **dimGeography** – City, country, and region mapping

---

Dashboards Included

Time Analysis Dashboard
Focuses on **sales performance over time**.

**Key Insights:**
- Total Quantity, Revenue, COGS, Profit & Profit Margin
- Year-wise transaction comparison (2005–2008)
- Monthly profit trends
- Weekday vs Weekend contribution
- Quarterly profit distribution
- Territory-based performance

**Highlights:**
- Selected months in 2005 contributed **31.9% of total profit**
- Weekdays contributed **72% of total profit**
- Q2 generated the highest quarterly profit

---

Detail Dashboard
Provides **deep-dive business insights**.

**Key Insights:**
- Top 5 most profitable products
- Profit share: Top-5 products vs others
- Product profit by color
- Expensive vs less-expensive product contribution
- Top customers by profit
- Profit by gender and age group
- Country-wise profit contribution (USA & Australia leading)

**Highlights:**
- Products priced above $150 contributed **95.4% of total profit**
- Age group **50+ contributed 39.1% of profit**
- USA & Australia together contributed **62.7% of profit**
